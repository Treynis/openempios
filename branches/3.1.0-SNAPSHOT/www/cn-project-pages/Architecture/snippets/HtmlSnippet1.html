OpenEMPI Architecture - Version 2.0  

 

After the redesign and refactoring of OpenEMPI, the architecture of the software is now based on the principles of Service Oriented Architecture consisting of a number of independent services that are loosely coupled from the rest. Each service is exposed to other services through a well defined interface. Figure 1 illustrates the high level architecture of OpenEMPI bringing out the multi-layered architecture of the software. The diagram of Figure 1 is not an exhaustive list of all the components that comprise the system but only shows a subset of them for the purpose of illustrating the layered architecture of the system and to bring out the loose coupling between the services.

Figure 1. High Level Architecture of OpenEMPI and its Layers

Another benefit of the service oriented architecture of OpenEMPI is that since the key components like the matching and blocking algorithms are only exposed to the other services of the system through a well defined interface, alternate implementations of those components can be implemented and plugged in independently of the rest of the system. This capability is utilized in the implementation of matching algorithms. Currently OpenEMPI provides two distinct implementation of the matching algorithm. A deterministic exact version and a probabilistic one based on the Fellegi-Sunter algorithm. Depending of your requirements you can choose which version you prefer to use by simple changing the Spring configuration of the system.
